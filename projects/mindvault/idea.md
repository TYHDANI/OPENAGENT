# MindVault — AI Knowledge Engine & Decision Intelligence

## Problem
Knowledge workers consume 100+ hours of content monthly (podcasts, books, YouTube, articles, courses) but retain less than 10%. Naval Ravikant's "specific knowledge" — the unique expertise that makes you irreplaceable — compounds over time, but only if you can recall and connect it. No tool captures, organizes, and makes your accumulated knowledge actionable.

## Solution
A personal AI knowledge engine that turns everything you learn into a compounding intellectual advantage.
- **Universal Capture**: Ingest any content source — paste YouTube URLs (auto-transcribe), book highlights (Kindle sync), podcast episodes, article links, manual notes, voice memos, course notes. AI extracts key frameworks, mental models, and actionable insights automatically
- **Knowledge Graph**: Visual map of all your knowledge with AI-discovered connections between ideas across different sources. See how Naval's leverage concept connects to Hormozi's scaling framework connects to Dalio's cycle theory. Emergent insights you'd never find manually
- **Decision Engine**: Input any decision → MindVault pulls relevant frameworks from YOUR accumulated knowledge and generates a structured analysis. "Should I raise prices?" → pulls pricing frameworks from 3 books you read, 2 podcast episodes, and your own past analysis
- **Specific Knowledge Tracker**: Naval's concept productized — AI identifies where your knowledge is unique (specific knowledge) vs common (replaceable). Shows your intellectual moat and suggests areas to deepen
- **Weekly Knowledge Compound Rate**: Score showing how your knowledge base is growing, what themes are emerging, and what gaps exist
- **Framework Library**: Pre-loaded with 100+ business/life frameworks (MOAT scoring, First Principles, Inversion, Second-Order Thinking, Opportunity Cost) that you build on with your own examples

## Target Audience
Knowledge workers, entrepreneurs, executives, lifelong learners, podcast/audiobook consumers, students. People who consume content deliberately and want to compound their learning. 200M+ knowledge workers globally, 100M+ podcast listeners.

## Competition
- **Notion** ($10/mo): Note-taking, no AI knowledge extraction or decision engine
- **Readwise** ($8.99/mo): Highlight capture only, no frameworks, no decision engine
- **Mem.ai** ($10/mo): AI notes but no knowledge graph, no frameworks, no decision engine
- **Obsidian** (Free): Manual linking, no AI extraction, steep learning curve
- **None** combine auto-capture + knowledge graph + decision engine + specific knowledge tracking

## Notes
