## 1. Executive Summary

Origin is a regulated infrastructure platform for third-party reproduction -- matching intended parents with egg donors, sperm donors, and surrogates using AI compatibility scoring, integrated legal dashboards, milestone-based escrow, and clinic coordination. The MVP targets egg donation matching for intended parents in the US, with expansion to surrogacy (5-10x transaction values) after validation.

**Key Finding**: The fertility services market is valued at $39.88B (2025) growing at 9-10% CAGR to $87B+ by 2034. The egg donation segment alone is $3.4B globally ($398M in the US). No existing platform combines AI matching + legal automation + escrow + clinic integration in a single product. The opportunity is real, but regulatory complexity and trust barriers require careful execution.

## 4. Regulatory Landscape

### 4.1 US State-Level Regulation

Surrogacy and egg donation are primarily regulated at the state level. There is no single federal law governing surrogacy.

**Surrogacy-Friendly States (Phase 3 launch states):**
California, Connecticut, Delaware, District of Columbia, Maine, Nevada, New Hampshire, Oregon, Rhode Island, Washington

**Recent Legislative Changes:**
- Michigan legalized compensated surrogacy (April 1, 2025) -- [Hatch Fertility](https://www.hatch.us/en/blog/best-states-for-surrogacy)
- Tennessee codified IVF access (July 2025) -- [Ogletree](https://ogletree.com/insights-resources/blog-posts/state-laws-aim-to-reverse-baby-bust-and-cut-ivf-costs/)
- Georgia protected IVF access (HB 428, July 2025)
- California mandated large group insurance cover IVF (SB 729)

**States Where Surrogacy is Prohibited or Restricted:**
Nebraska and Louisiana prohibit compensated surrogacy. A handful of others have ambiguous or no explicit laws.

### 4.2 FDA Regulation

The FDA regulates reproductive tissue (eggs, sperm, embryos) under 21 CFR Parts 1270 and 1271. Key requirements:
- Donor eligibility screening and testing for communicable diseases (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, syphilis, etc.)
- Facilities handling reproductive tissue must register with the FDA
- Origin does NOT handle reproductive tissue directly -- it is a matching and coordination platform, reducing direct FDA exposure
- Partner clinics bear the regulatory burden for tissue handling

Source: [PVED - FDA Regulations for Egg Donation](https://pved.org/fda.php)

### 4.3 ASRM Guidelines

The American Society for Reproductive Medicine (ASRM) provides ethical and clinical guidance:
- Egg donor compensation should be fair and not constitute undue inducement
- No explicit dollar cap, but compensation should reflect time, inconvenience, and discomfort
- Screening standards for donors (medical, psychological, genetic)
- Repetitive donation limits

Source: [ASRM Ethics Committee](https://www.asrm.org/practice-guidance/ethics-opinions/financial-compensation-of-oocyte-donors-an-ethics-committee-opinion/)

### 4.4 HIPAA and Privacy

**Critical Development**: The HIPAA Reproductive Health Rule (2024) was vacated by the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas on June 18, 2025. This means:
- No special HIPAA protections for reproductive health data beyond standard HIPAA
- Standard HIPAA Privacy Rule, Security Rule, and Breach Notification Rule still apply
- Remaining NPP modifications due by February 16, 2026

Source: [Holland & Knight](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/06/hipaas-reproductive-health-rule-is-vacated-nationally)

**Origin's HIPAA Position:**
- Origin is likely a **Business Associate** (not a Covered Entity) if it processes PHI on behalf of clinics
- If Origin operates independently (no clinic integration), it may not be a HIPAA-covered entity, but should still adopt HIPAA-grade security as best practice
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) required with all clinic partners
- State-level privacy laws (CCPA/CPRA in California, state health data laws) also apply

### 4.5 FTC Considerations

- Health Breach Notification Rule applies to non-HIPAA-covered health apps
- Deceptive advertising rules apply to any claims about matching success rates
- Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) may apply if donor-conceived children use the platform later

## 9. Key Insights and Recommendations

### 9.1 Why This Works
1. **Massive market, minimal tech disruption**: $40B+ fertility market with agencies charging $20K-$40K fees using 1990s processes. Origin can capture $3K-$8K per journey at 80% lower cost to parents.
2. **Strong unit economics**: 70-85% gross margins, LTV:CAC of 3-8x, payback in 2-4 months.
3. **Network effects**: More donors attract more intended parents, and vice versa. Clinic partnerships create distribution moats.
4. **Regulatory tailwind**: More states legalizing surrogacy (Michigan 2025), more insurance mandating IVF coverage (California SB 729). Demand is growing and legal barriers are falling.
5. **VC validation**: Cofertility's $16M raise proves investor appetite. FemTech funding up 55% YoY to $2.6B.

### 9.2 Why This is Hard
1. **Trust is everything**: People are making life-defining decisions. A single safety incident could destroy the brand.
2. **Regulatory patchwork**: 50 states with different surrogacy laws, plus international variation. Requires ongoing legal investment.
3. **Long sales cycles**: Fertility journeys take 3-18 months. Revenue recognition is delayed.
4. **Ethical scrutiny**: Reproductive matching invites media and regulatory attention. Ethics board and transparency are non-negotiable.
5. **Clinic integration is slow**: FHIR/HL7 integrations with fertility EMRs take 3-6 months each.

### 9.3 Strategic Recommendations
1. **Launch egg donation only** (lowest regulatory complexity, fastest path to revenue)
2. **Partner with 3-5 fertility clinics** in CA, NY, TX for launch distribution
3. **Hire reproductive law counsel** before writing a single line of code
4. **Establish ethics board** with fertility medicine, bioethics, and patient advocacy representation
5. **Build SOC 2 compliance from Day 1** (not retrofit later)
6. **Content marketing first**: SEO for "how to find an egg donor", "egg donation process", "egg donor matching" -- these are high-intent, low-competition keywords
7. **Web + iOS**: Consider a web app (Next.js) alongside iOS to maximize reach. Many intended parents research on desktop.
